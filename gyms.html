Pewter City — Brock

A strong opening. Brock’s Geodude and Onix are both Rock/Ground, making them extremely vulnerable to your Grass attacks. They can barely scratch you in return.

Cerulean City — Misty

Misty’s Water-types—Staryu and Starmie—struggle to do any damage, while Bulbasaur can take them down with ease. A clear advantage.

Vermilion City — Lt. Surge

Surge’s Electric-types hit lightly thanks to your resistance. You won’t deal super-effective damage, but you’re safe and steady here.

Celadon City — Erika

Erika’s team is mostly Grass/Poison, which resists your attacks and can drag the fight out. Neither side has a clear edge, but it’s a slow match.

Fuchsia City — Koga

Koga’s Poison-types are dangerous. While you resist some of their secondary attacks, Bulbasaur’s Poison typing leaves it exposed to Psychic moves and status effects. Not a great matchup.

Saffron City — Sabrina

A serious threat. Sabrina’s Psychic-types strike directly at your Poison typing. You’re at a heavy disadvantage here and will need strong allies to cover this gap.

Cinnabar Island — Blaine

This is your worst matchup. Blaine’s team is fast and built around powerful Fire moves that tear through you. Avoid using Bulbasaur if possible.

Viridian City — Giovanni

A strong finish. Giovanni’s Ground-heavy team is weak to Grass, and Bulbasaur can dominate if you watch out for any Fire-type coverage. One of your better matchups.

That’s the full Gym circuit, sis!

With your partner Bulbasaur, you’ve faced tough matchups and emerged stronger each time.

But the real challenge still lies ahead...

So how does Bulbasaur match up against the Elite Four?



- Lorelei (Ice/Water): mixed. You resist Water but Ice is deadly. Not a good lead.
- Bruno (Fighting/Rock): decent. Watch out for coverage, but Grass helps vs. Onix.
- Agatha (Ghost/Poison): poor. Bulbasaur has no advantage here.
- Lance (Dragon/Flying): very bad. You’re weak to Flying and can't hit back effectively.



Consider rotating your team before the League — Bulbasaur carried early, but won’t sweep the endgame alone.
